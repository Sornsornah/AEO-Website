# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Company employees** browsing the About, Products, and Blog pages — broad internal audience, casual reading context, likely on a laptop at their desk during work hours. They want to understand what the AI Enablement Office does and what they've built.

**C-suite executives** accessing the restricted /updates section — time-constrained, skimming monthly achievement reports. They need confidence and clarity, not depth-first navigation.

**PMs (admins)** who publish updates, write blog posts, and manage product entries — power users of the editor and admin surfaces, focused on efficiency and accuracy.

## Product Purpose

UpdateCentral is the AI Enablement Office's public face inside the company. It communicates what the department does (About), what they've built (Products), what they've learned (Blog), and reports monthly achievements to C-suite stakeholders (Internal Updates). Success means the company understands and trusts the AEO's work — and C-suite readers leave each visit with a clear picture of progress.

## Brand Personality

Considered, editorial, unhurried. Like a well-produced internal magazine that takes its work seriously without taking itself too seriously. Three words: **clear, confident, purposeful**.

Reference: Notion — clean typographic hierarchy, generous whitespace, functional restraint, no visual noise. The interface recedes so the content leads.

## Anti-references

- Generic HR intranet aesthetics (gray sidebars, accordion menus, icon-heavy navigation)
- Confluence-style wiki layouts (cramped, utilitarian, corporate)
- Heavy-serif newspaper or report templates (this is a living site, not a PDF)
- SaaS-startup hero sections with gradient blobs and feature-grid cards

## Design Principles

1. **Content leads, interface recedes.** Every decorative element earns its place by serving the content. When in doubt, remove it.
2. **Editorial confidence.** Use whitespace actively. Strong typographic hierarchy does the work that color and decoration often try to cover.
3. **Unified across surfaces.** Brand pages (About, Products, Blog) and product surfaces (Updates, Editor) share the same token system. The product surfaces simplify rather than diverge.
4. **Premium without pretension.** Polished and considered, but never stiff or corporate. The tone is smart and collegial.
5. **Clarity at every zoom level.** C-suite readers skim. Regular readers browse. PMs scan editor tables. Every screen should communicate its key point within 3 seconds.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Looks good and readable is the baseline. Maintain sufficient contrast ratios for body text and interactive elements. No specific WCAG level mandated, but WCAG AA for text contrast is a sensible floor.
